MENU Retro Walk 2019 WRITE

ELDERS FACE SUBTLE POSTURAL IMBALANCE THAT RAISES FALL RISK DURING EVERYDAY STANDING AND WALKING. CLINIC TOOLS MISS REAL-LIFE PATTERNS, AND CURRENT WEARABLES MEASURE BUT DON’T COACH, LEAVING ACTION GAPS. WE NEED A COMFORTABLE SMART INSOLE THAT SENSES, EXPLAINS, AND GUIDES

Objective The Project
Design
Prototype

Retro Walk is a closed-loop coaching insole + app for elders: a load-cell grid (100–200 Hz) detects a posture error and instantly launches a 15–30 s micro-tutorial on the phone (animation/voice). While the user follows, the insole compares live pressure/COP to a target pattern to verify correction, then returns a simple pass / try-again score with one-line tips and logs the attempt. The system targets less than 50 ms latency, a 3-step calibration (sit–stand–lean), all-day comfort, and an optional caregiver summary—measure → guide → confirm.

THE PROCESS

We listed the main use-cases and defined what “success” meant. We chose the sensor layout and parts (chip, battery, IMU) and kept delay and power low. We coded fast sampling with a simple 3-step setup. When posture was wrong, the app showed a 15–30 s tutorial, then the insole checked the pressure pattern and said pass/try again. We bench-tested, compared with a force plate, ran a short home pilot, kept data on the device, and improved to V2.

THE PROCESS
