MENU RETRO WALK — POSTURE COACH 2024 WRITE

Retro Walk is a smart insole and posture coach for older adults. It turns tiny shifts in foot pressure into simple visual cues, so people can notice early imbalance and keep a steadier, safer walk over time.

Context

M.Des thesis · Assistive mobility for women with lower-limb disability.

Role

Researcher & designer · ethnography, mapping, prototyping, user studies.

Tools

Pressure sensors, Arduino, Grasshopper / Rhino, Figma, Excel.

Objective

Design a low-friction system that makes postural risk legible in daily life: capture pressure patterns while walking, surface them as gentle prompts, and learn how elders actually react to these cues in their homes.

Field research & mapping
Posture & pressure infographic

This visual explains how pressure travels across the foot during a step, and where instability quietly creeps in. I used it with participants to talk about their own gait in simple language.

Prototyping the insole & coach
What I studied
  • How posture drift shows up in pressure plots.
  • Which visual cues elders actually notice and trust.
  • When coaching feels supportive vs. nagging.
Key insights
  • People respond better to micro-tutorials (“two steady steps now”) than to generic warnings.
  • A slow, warm colour shift is read as “doing okay”; harsh blinking is read as scolding and quickly ignored.
  • Elders wanted a way to “see progress” over a week, not just be told what is wrong in the moment.
Outcome

Retro Walk became a working prototype that links gait data, subtle coaching and elders’ lived routines. It now feeds into a larger thesis on low-floor mobility and domestic independence for women with lower-limb disability.
